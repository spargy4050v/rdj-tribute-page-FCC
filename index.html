After their divorce in 2004, Downey remarried in 2005 to producer Susan Downey(fomerly Susan Levin).Together, they have two children, Exton and Avri. He was placed on probation for 3 years but struggled to comply with court-ordered drug testing and rehabilitation programs.       Setbacks (1996-2001)
      • Loss of Roles:

        During his years of struggle, Downey missed out on numerous potential roles that could have futhered his career. Directors and producers were reluctant to cast him fearing the complications his legal and personal issues could bring, which made him a liability to cast for them. These missed opportunities stunted his career growth, leading to a period of relative obscurity.

      • "Ally McBeal" Firing:

        One of the notable setbacks was his dismissal from the television show "Ally McBeal" in 2001 after being arrested for drug possession while on probation. This role had provided him with a much-needed career boost, earning him a Gloden Globe. His dismissal not only cut short this career resurgence but also signaled to the industry that he was a high-risk actor.

      • Insurance Issues:

        Downey's history of substance abuse made it difficult for film studios to insure him, which is a standard requirement for lead actors in major productions. Insurance companies were unwilling to cover him, making it financially risky for studios to cast him in significant roles. This added another layer of complexity to securing roles, which effectively barredd him from many mainstream projects as studios were reluctant to take the financial risk, by limiting him to smaller, independent films that were willing to take the risk.
